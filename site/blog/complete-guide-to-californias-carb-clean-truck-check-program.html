Complete Guide to California's CARB Clean Truck Check Program

Published August 18, 2025 · NorCal CARB Mobile

What Every Truck Owner Needs to Know in 2025

If you operate a diesel truck weighing over 14,000 lbs GVWR in California, the Clean Truck Check (CTC) isn't optional—it's the law. This comprehensive guide breaks down everything you need to know about staying compliant and avoiding costly penalties.

What is the Clean Truck Check?

The Clean Truck Check is California's mandatory inspection program for heavy-duty diesel vehicles. Implemented by the California Air Resources Board (CARB), this program ensures that emission control systems on diesel trucks are functioning properly. Think of it as an advanced smog check specifically designed for commercial vehicles.

Who Needs a Clean Truck Check?

Your vehicle requires a Clean Truck Check if it meets these criteria:

  • Diesel-powered

  • Gross Vehicle Weight Rating (GVWR) of 14,001 lbs or more

  • Operating in California (even if registered out-of-state)

  • Model year 2013 or newer (as of 2025)

This includes semi-trucks, box trucks, dump trucks, tow trucks, and many service vehicles. Even if you're just passing through California for deliveries, you're required to comply.

Testing Frequency and Deadlines

Vehicles must be tested twice per year, with inspections at least 90 days apart. The specific testing periods depend on your license plate's last digit:

  • Plates ending in 1-5: January 1 - June 30 and July 1 - December 31

  • Plates ending in 6-0: April 1 - September 30 and October 1 - March 31

Missing your testing window results in automatic non-compliance, potentially leading to DMV registration holds and DOT citations.

What Happens During the Test?

The inspection takes approximately 15-20 minutes and includes:

  • Connecting to your vehicle's OBD port

  • Running diagnostic scans of emission control systems

  • Checking for active fault codes

  • Verifying emission equipment functionality

  • Visual inspection of exhaust components

Consequences of Non-Compliance

Failing to maintain Clean Truck Check compliance can result in:

  • $1,000+ roadside citations

  • DMV registration blocks

  • DOT operating authority suspension

  • Inability to renew commercial insurance

  • Loss of contracts requiring CARB compliance
