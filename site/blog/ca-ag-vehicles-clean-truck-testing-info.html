Do California Agricultural Vehicles Need Clean Truck Check Testing? Yes—Here’s What You Need to Know

Published June 25, 2025 · NorCal CARB Mobile

If you operate agricultural trucks in California, it’s not just smart to stay compliant with emissions testing—it’s the law. Under California’s Clean Truck Check (Heavy-Duty Inspection and Maintenance Program), most agricultural vehicles are required to undergo annual emissions testing, even if they only run seasonally or locally.

At NorCal CARB Mobile, we make it easy by bringing certified Clean Truck Check testing right to your ranch, farm, or facility. No hauling. No downtime. Just fast, compliant testing—on your schedule.

🌾 What Is the Clean Truck Check Program?

California’s Clean Truck Check is a statewide regulation requiring emissions testing for most diesel and alternative-fuel heavy-duty vehicles with a GVWR over 14,000 lbs. That includes many agricultural trucks, especially:

  • Grain haulers

  • Livestock carriers

  • Flatbeds and stake trucks used for crop transport

  • Truck-tractor and trailer combinations hauling to the first point of processing

Even if your vehicle is only used seasonally or stays on rural roads, you still may be required to test annually.

🧑‍🌾 What Is an Agricultural Vehicle—And Does It Need Testing?

An agricultural vehicle is defined by the California Air Resources Board (CARB) as any vehicle:

  • Owned or operated by a farming business, and

  • Used exclusively for agricultural operations, such as transporting farm products to the first point of processing

🛑 Personal-use vehicles, general transport vehicles, and those not directly supporting farming do not qualify for agricultural exemption status and still need testing if they meet Clean Truck Check criteria.

✔️ If your ag vehicle operates in California and weighs over 14,000 lbs, chances are it needs to be tested annually.
