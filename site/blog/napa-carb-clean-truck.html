Understanding CARB Compliance in Napa County

California's Air Resources Board (CARB) established the Heavy-Duty Inspection and Maintenance (HDIM) Program, commonly known as the Clean Truck Check, to reduce emissions from commercial diesel vehicles. If your Napa Valley business operates trucks with a Gross Vehicle Weight Rating (GVWR) over 14,000 pounds, compliance testing is mandatory every year.

Non-compliance carries serious consequences:

  • $300 per vehicle per day in fines for operating without valid compliance

  • Registration holds preventing vehicle renewal

  • Potential impoundment during roadside inspections

  • Lost business opportunities and revenue

For Napa businesses operating on tight margins—from boutique wineries to agricultural cooperatives—these penalties can significantly impact your bottom line.

Common CARB Compliance Questions from Napa Businesses

Q: How often does my vehicle need testing?
A: Heavy-duty diesel vehicles must be tested annually. We recommend scheduling your test 60 days before your registration renewal date to allow time for any necessary repairs.

Q: What if my vehicle fails inspection?
A: We provide detailed diagnostic information about why a vehicle failed. Many issues can be addressed by local mechanics, and we're happy to retest once repairs are completed—at your location.

Q: Do I need to be present during testing?
A: While we need access to the vehicle, you don't need to be present for the entire test. Many clients provide keys and continue their normal operations while we work.

Q: Can you test multiple vehicles in one visit?
A: Absolutely. Fleet testing is one of our specialties. We'll coordinate a schedule that tests your entire fleet efficiently, often completing multiple vehicles in a single visit.

Q: What areas of Napa Valley do you serve?
A: We provide mobile testing throughout all of Napa County, including Napa, American Canyon, Yountville, Oakville, Rutherford, St. Helena, and Calistoga.
