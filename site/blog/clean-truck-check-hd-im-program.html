Clean Truck Check (HD I/M) Program Study Guide

  1. What is the primary objective of California's Clean Truck Check (HD I/M) program?

  2. Which legislative bill mandated the creation of the Clean Truck Check program, and when was it approved by CARB?

  3. What are the key components or strategies that the Clean Truck Check program employs to achieve its goals?

  4. When did the implementation of the Clean Truck Check program begin, and what is its expected outcome when fully implemented?

  5. What is the role of NORCAL CARB MOBILE in the context of the Clean Truck Check program?

  6. Describe the unique selling proposition of NORCAL CARB MOBILE's service for heavy-duty vehicle owners.

  7. How does NORCAL CARB MOBILE incentivize referrals for the Clean Truck Check service?

  8. What is the minimum cost for large fleets using NORCAL CARB MOBILE's services?

  9. Besides periodic vehicle testing, what other emissions monitoring techniques does the Clean Truck Check program incorporate?

  10. Who is the primary contact for inquiries regarding the Clean Truck Check (HD I/M) program at the California Air Resources Board?

Answer Key

  1. What is the primary objective of California's Clean Truck Check (HD I/M) program? The primary objective of the Clean Truck Check program is to ensure that heavy-duty vehicles' emissions control systems are properly functioning when traveling on California's roadways. This is crucial for achieving significant reductions in smog-forming and carcinogenic toxic air pollution.

  2. Which legislative bill mandated the creation of the Clean Truck Check program, and when was it approved by CARB? Senate Bill 210 (Leyva; Chapter 298, Statutes of 2019) directed CARB to develop and implement this regulation. The Board approved the regulation on December 9, 2021.

  3. What are the key components or strategies that the Clean Truck Check program employs to achieve its goals? The program combines periodic vehicle testing requirements with other emissions monitoring techniques. It also includes expanded enforcement strategies to identify vehicles needing emissions-related repairs and ensures those repairs are performed.

  4. When did the implementation of the Clean Truck Check program begin, and what is its expected outcome when fully implemented? Implementation of the Clean Truck Check program began phasing in starting January 2023. When fully implemented, it is expected to provide significant reductions in smog-forming and carcinogenic toxic air pollution, helping achieve federal air quality mandates and healthy air in California.

  5. What is the role of NORCAL CARB MOBILE in the context of the Clean Truck Check program? NORCAL CARB MOBILE provides a mobile Clean Truck Check inspection service for Northern California, offering a convenient way for heavy-duty vehicle owners to meet CARB and Clean Truck Check requirements without needing to visit a physical shop.

  6. Describe the unique selling proposition of NORCAL CARB MOBILE's service for heavy-duty vehicle owners. Their unique selling proposition is bringing certified technicians directly to the customer's location, whether home or work, to perform inspections. This offers convenience, transparent pricing, and reliable service, aimed at building lasting trust.

  7. How does NORCAL CARB MOBILE incentivize referrals for the Clean Truck Check service? NORCAL CARB MOBILE offers a $10 Digital Visa Gift Card for each qualified Clean Truck Check referral that completes testing with them. There is no limit to how many cards a referrer can earn.

  8. What is the minimum cost for large fleets using NORCAL CARB MOBILE's services? NORCAL CARB MOBILE advertises transparent pricing starting as low as $50 for large fleets.

  9. Besides periodic vehicle testing, what other emissions monitoring techniques does the Clean Truck Check program incorporate? In addition to periodic vehicle testing, the Clean Truck Check program incorporates "other emissions monitoring techniques." While specific techniques aren't fully detailed in the provided text, On-Board Diagnostics (OBD) is mentioned as a related program.

  10. Who is the primary contact for inquiries regarding the Clean Truck Check (HD I/M) program at the California Air Resources Board? The primary contact for the Clean Truck Check (HD I/M) program at CARB is hdim@arb.ca.gov via email or phone at (866) 634-3735.

Glossary of Key Terms

  • California Air Resources Board (CARB): A department within the California Environmental Protection Agency responsible for maintaining and improving air quality, and controlling pollution from various sources, including vehicles.

  • Clean Truck Check (HD I/M): The official name for California's Heavy-Duty Vehicle Inspection and Maintenance program, designed to ensure proper functioning of emissions control systems in heavy-duty vehicles.

  • Heavy-Duty Vehicle Inspection and Maintenance (HD I/M): The comprehensive regulatory program developed by CARB to reduce emissions from heavy-duty vehicles through testing, monitoring, and enforcement.

  • Senate Bill 210 (Leyva; Chapter 298, Statutes of 2019): The specific California legislation that directed CARB to develop and implement the Clean Truck Check (HD I/M) regulation.

  • Emissions Control Systems: Components and technologies in a vehicle designed to reduce the output of harmful pollutants from the exhaust.

  • Smog-forming Pollution: Air pollutants that react in the atmosphere to create ground-level ozone, a primary component of smog, which can harm human health and vegetation.

  • Carcinogenic Toxic Air Pollution: Air pollutants that are known or suspected to cause cancer in humans.

  • Federal Air Quality Mandates: Standards and requirements set by the U.S. Environmental Protection Agency (EPA) for ambient air quality, which states must meet to protect public health and welfare.

  • Periodic Vehicle Testing: Regular, scheduled inspections of vehicles to ensure their emissions control systems are functioning correctly and they meet emissions standards.

  • Emissions Monitoring Techniques: Methods used to track and assess the level of pollutants emitted by vehicles, which can include on-board diagnostics (OBD) and other remote sensing technologies.

  • On-Board Diagnostics (OBD) Program: A system in vehicles that monitors the performance of the engine's components, including those responsible for controlling emissions, and alerts the driver to malfunctions.

  • Credentialed Testers: Individuals who have received specific training and certification to perform Clean Truck Check inspections.

  • Referee Program: A component of the Clean Truck Check program (and similar I/M programs) that provides expert assistance and dispute resolution for complex emissions-related issues or failed tests.

  • NORCAL CARB MOBILE: A private mobile service provider offering Clean Truck Check inspections for heavy-duty vehicles in Northern California.

  • J1667 Smoke Opacity Testing: A specific test method often used for diesel vehicles to measure the opacity (darkness) of exhaust smoke, indicating particulate matter emissions.
