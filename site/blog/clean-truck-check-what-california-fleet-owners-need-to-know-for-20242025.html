What Is Clean Truck Check?

Clean Truck Check (CTC) is California's required emissions inspection and maintenance program for heavy-duty vehicles over 14,000 lbs GVWR. It ensures trucks, buses, and fleets comply with state air quality standards.

If your vehicle operates in California—even if registered in another state—CTC likely applies.

Covered Vehicles Include:

  • Diesel and alternative-fuel trucks

  • School, shuttle, and transit buses

  • Commercial hybrids

  • Government fleet vehicles

  • California-registered motorhomes

Do Any Vehicles Qualify for Exemption?

Yes. The following are not subject to Clean Truck Check:

  • Battery-electric and hydrogen vehicles

  • Tactical military vehicles

  • Emergency and historical vehicles

  • Motorhomes registered out-of-state

  • Engines certified to the cleanest NOx standard (exempt until 2027)

If you're unsure about your fleet status, call NorCal CARB Mobile for a quick eligibility review.

What’s Happening Now?

CARB began enforcement in January 2023 using roadside emissions monitoring. If your vehicle is flagged, you’ll receive a Notice to Submit to Testing (NST).

From October 1, 2024, emissions testing will be required for compliance deadlines on or after January 1, 2025. Vehicles must pass and submit a certified test within the required timeframe.

Keep Your Records Accurate in CTC-VIS

Every fleet owner is responsible for updating their data in the Clean Truck Check system:

  • Add new vehicles within 30 days

  • Update temporary or missing license plates

  • Remove sold or inactive vehicles
