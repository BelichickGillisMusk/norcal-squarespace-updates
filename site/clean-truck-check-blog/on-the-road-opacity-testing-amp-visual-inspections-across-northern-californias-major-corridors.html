On the Road: Opacity Testing & Visual Inspections Across Northern California's Major Corridors

Published August 28, 2025 · NorCal CARB Mobile

If you operate diesel vehicles in California, you're likely familiar with the strict emissions standards set by the California Air Resources Board (CARB). At NorCal CarB Mobile, we bring these essential compliance services directly to your location throughout major cities along I-5 and I-80, saving you valuable time and ensuring your fleet stays operational and compliant.

What is Opacity Testing?

Opacity testing measures the darkness or density of exhaust smoke coming from diesel vehicles. In simple terms, it determines how much light can pass through the exhaust emissions. Higher opacity readings indicate darker smoke, which typically means higher levels of particulate matter pollution—something CARB regulations aim to minimize.

Our mobile opacity testing equipment uses light-sensing technology to precisely measure this opacity percentage. California regulations typically require most diesel vehicles to maintain opacity levels below 5%, with some older vehicles allowed slightly higher thresholds.

What are Visual Inspections?

Visual inspections are complementary to opacity tests and involve a trained technician examining your vehicle's emission control systems to ensure all components are present, properly connected, and functioning as designed. During a visual inspection, we check for:

  • Tampering or modifications to emission control systems

  • Proper function of the Exhaust Gas Recirculation (EGR) system

  • Diesel Particulate Filter (DPF) condition

  • Proper labeling and engine certification

  • Evidence of excessive smoke or fluid leaks

Regular opacity testing and visual inspections aren't just about avoiding fines—they're essential for maintaining your operating authority and ensuring your vehicles aren't sidelined due to compliance issues.
