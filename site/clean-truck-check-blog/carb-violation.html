Received a CARB Violation Letter? 3 Steps to Avoid a $1,000+ Fine Today Category: Compliance Alerts

Published January 18, 2026 · NorCal CARB Mobile

If a recent letter from the California Air Resources Board (CARB) or a DMV registration renewal notice has flagged your heavy-duty truck as non-compliant, you are officially in a compliance crisis. With CARB actively enforcing mandates and placing registration holds, delay is your most expensive decision. Non-compliance can lead to fines ranging from $1,000 to $75,000+ per violation.

The clock is ticking. Here are the three critical steps you must take right now to avoid fines and a crippling DMV registration hold:

Step 1: Confirm Your Status and Pay the Fee

Log into your Clean Truck Check-Vehicle Inspection System (CTC-VIS) account. Non-compliance is often triggered by failing to register in the system or failing to pay the annual compliance fee ($30/vehicle). Ensure this fee is paid immediately.

Step 2: Stop Driving and Call for Emergency Mobile Testing

A non-compliant vehicle is not legally allowed to operate on California public roads. The fastest way to restore compliance is to schedule an emissions test with a CARB-credentialed provider immediately.

Why waste hours driving to a testing station when your vehicle is already at risk? NorCal CARB Mobile brings certified testing directly to your location—your yard, your job site, or your maintenance facility—reducing downtime and eliminating the risk of being cited while en route.

Step 3: Get Your Results Uploaded (This is Key!)

A passing test result must be submitted to CARB. We handle this critical step instantly. Vehicles that comply with Clean Truck Check are sent to the DMV daily, and it typically takes about three business days for the DMV to process and reflect the vehicle as compliant, clearing your registration hold.
