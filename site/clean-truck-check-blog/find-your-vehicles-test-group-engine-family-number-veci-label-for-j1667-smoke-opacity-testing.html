Find Your Vehicle’s “Test Group” / “Engine Family” Number (VECI Label) for J1667 Smoke Opacity Testing

Why this number matters for J1667 opacity testing

Every U.S. vehicle has a drivetrain identifier called the Test Group or Engine Family number. It tells us the precise emissions configuration your vehicle was built to meet and ensures we bring the right gear and follow the correct procedure for your California diesel smoke opacity (SAE J1667) inspection—especially important for motorhomes (RVs), heavy-duty pickups, and commercial diesels.

Where to find the label (VECI)

Look for the Vehicle Emission Control Information (VECI) label in the engine area. Common locations:

  • Underside of the hood (most common)

  • Radiator support/core support

  • Engine cover or air intake shroud (rear-engine motorhomes often place it there)

Example text you may see:

VEHICLE EMISSION CONTROL INFORMATION
TEST GROUP: 3CXYX05.4VKR
ENGINE FAMILY: XYZ123ABC456
DISPLACEMENT: 5.9L
EVAP FAMILY: 3CXYX0145KVT

We need the value labeled TEST GROUP or ENGINE FAMILY.

Tip: Snap a clear phone photo of the entire label and email it to support@norcalcarbmobile.com with your appointment time.

Can’t find it?

Motorhomes and some heavy-duty engines can be tricky. If the label is missing, painted over, or inaccessible:

  1. Check your owner’s manual for a label diagram.

  2. Send us engine bay photos—we can often spot it remotely.

  3. Share your VIN and engine brand—we can request the engine family name from the manufacturer (e.g., Cummins).

Pre-appointment checklist (avoid delays & automatic fails)

  • ✅ Locate and photograph the VECI label (Test Group / Engine Family).

  • ✅ Make sure the area is clean and readable (wipe dust/oil).

  • ✅ Provide VIN and engine make/model if the label isn’t visible.

  • ✅ Have the vehicle parked safely, warmed up, and accessible for SAE J1667 testing.

If the label isn’t visible at time of service, we may not be able to complete the test on-site.

FAQs: Engine Family / Test Group & J1667

What if my label says only “Test Group” or only “Engine Family”?
They’re equivalent for our purposes. Send whichever you see.

My RV has a rear engine—where’s the label?
Check the engine cover, air intake, or a panel near the rear service bay. If you still can’t find it, send photos.

Can you test without the Engine Family/Test Group?
We need it in advance. If it isn’t visible, we’ll try to source it using your VIN and engine details, but that may require rescheduling.
