Staying Compliant in Sacramento County: How NorCalCARBMobile.com Keeps California Truckers and Fleets On the Road

California’s Clean Truck Check (formerly known as the HD I/M program) is now a mandatory part of owning and operating diesel trucks over 14,000 lbs in the state. If you're based in Sacramento County, this means staying compliant with CARB (California Air Resources Board) and the DMV is no longer optional — it's essential.

For trucking companies, fleet managers, and owner-operators, meeting these requirements can be a major disruption. That’s where NorCalCARBMobile.com steps in.

What is the Clean Truck Check?

The Clean Truck Check requires all heavy-duty diesel vehicles over 14,000 lbs GVWR to undergo regular SAE J1667 smoke opacity testing and visual emissions inspections. If you're late or fail to comply, your DMV registration can be blocked — leading to costly downtime and potential penalties.

This includes vehicles in a wide range of industries, from agriculture and construction to last-mile logistics and public works.
